How to Check Word and Character Counts for SEO & Editorial

Every marketing, editorial and SEO workflow revolves around length constraints. Title tags should stay under ~60 characters, meta descriptions around 155, email subject lines roughly 40, and social posts vary by platform. Instead of guessing or relying on heavyweight apps, you can track everything inside a lightweight, privacy-first counter like the Toolsivo Word & Character Counter.

Tip: keep the Toolsivo counter open in a dedicated browser tab while writing. You’ll avoid context switching and cut down on editing time.
Ad space

Why counts matter

Length constraints influence click-through rates, readability, and compliance. Google truncates long titles, email apps chop subject lines, and some ad networks reject creatives that exceed character limits. Tracking counts also helps you optimize reading time: a 2-minute article typically outperforms a 10-minute one in top-of-funnel campaigns.

Step-by-step process

  1. Open the counter: go to the Word & Character Counter tool.
  2. Paste your draft: use the main textarea for the body copy. Stats update instantly.
  3. Use the SEO fields: the tool provides dedicated inputs for title tags and meta descriptions with color-coded status.
  4. Check reading time: the helper uses a 200 wpm baseline. Adjust your draft if you target specific session lengths.
  5. Refine SERP preview: the preview shows how your title/description combo might appear in Google results.
  6. Iterate: tweak the copy until stats fall into the desired range, then copy/paste into your CMS.
Ad space

Channel-specific guidelines

Google Search

  • Titles: 50–60 characters.
  • Meta descriptions: 140–160 characters.
  • Use the preview area to avoid truncation.

Email marketing

  • Subject lines: 35–45 characters for mobile.
  • Preheader text: 40–70 characters.
  • Use the reading-time metric to cap newsletters at 3–5 minutes.

Social media

  • LinkedIn updates: 150 characters before the “see more” break.
  • Twitter/X: 280 characters total.
  • Instagram captions: 125 characters visible without tap.

Best practices

  • Write freely first: draft without constraints, then paste into the counter for trimming.
  • Use consistent baselines: align your entire team on the same limits to speed up approvals.
  • Document your ranges: keep a column in your content brief for “title chars,” “meta chars,” and “reading time.”
  • Save templates: copy the SERP preview output into briefs to show stakeholders how their copy will appear.

FAQ

Does the counter store my text?

No. It runs entirely in your browser tab. Closing the tab clears all data.

Can I change the reading-time baseline?

The default uses 200 words per minute—a common standard. For more precise calculations, multiply your word count by your preferred wpm.

How do I get multiple previews?

You can duplicate the page in a new tab for each draft or copy your stats into a spreadsheet. The counter is intentionally lightweight.
